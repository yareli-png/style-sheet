Getting Started

This is a Netflix's design style reference, readable by both humans and AI tools. No design background needed: pick what you need, copy it, and use it directly in your prompts.

Step by step

How to use this style sheet

1. Choose your setup
Pick your scheme and background: dark for most Netflix projects, light for docs and emails.
2. Browse and pick
Use the tabs to find what you need: Colors for flat colors, Gradients for backgrounds, Typography for text styles.
3. Grab what you need
Hover over any card and click the copy icon for a single value. To collect several, click the cards to pin them to the bar at the bottom.
4. Take it with you
Hit Copy to get everything in one block, or Download to save a file you can share or attach directly to an AI prompt.

FAQ

Quick answers to the most common questions about using this style sheet.

Getting started
What are the named colors and styles?
Each color, gradient, and text style in this tool has a name. Use that name instead of memorizing exact codes. It keeps your work consistent and makes it easy to reference in prompts or handoffs.
I'm not a designer. Can I still use this?
Absolutely. This tool is for everyone at Netflix who makes visual content. No design background needed.
Schemes and modes
What's the difference between Core, Cool, and Warm?
Three schemes, each with a different feel. Core is the classic red look. Cool is blue-toned, for product and tech. Warm is orange-toned, for creator contexts. Pick one and stick with it.
When should I use Dark vs Light mode?
Dark for anything that looks like Netflix. Light for docs, emails, and internal tools. Not sure? Drop us a message on Slack!
Using the tool
How do I copy a color?
Hover over any card and click the copy icon. The value lands on your clipboard, ready to paste.
What is the pin bar at the bottom?
Your collection tray. Click cards to save them, then hit Copy to grab everything at once or Download to save a file you can share or attach directly to an AI prompt.
Colors and typography
Why are there different color categories?
Each one has a purpose. Primary for brand colors, Secondary for backgrounds, Bright for expressive accents, Text for type colors, and Status Colors for links, errors, success, and warnings. Using the right one keeps things consistent.
Can I use any color on this page?
Yes, everything here is approved. Just don't bring in colors from outside this set. Need something you don't see? Let us know on Slack!
What are the text styles for?
Each text style is ready to use: font, size, weight, and spacing all in one. Pin and Download it just like a color.
